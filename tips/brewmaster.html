Brewmaster

Tips

  • You always get the Brewed Up buff when casting Cinder Brew, your hero does not need to be in the target AoE.
  • Know that critical attacks always roll during the beginning of an attack animation. You can't swap into Fire Brawler stance and get the crit mid-attack animation.
    • However, slowing effects are applied on attack impact. So if you have Agh's Shard and wanna get real sweaty, you can swap out of Fire Brawler after an attack starts and into Void Brawler to also get the slow on impact.
  • Void Brawler's slow works on spell immune enemies.
  • Rubick gets an error if he tries to Spell Steal you after you've toggled Drunken Brawler, so mash your stances when playing against a Rubick.
  • Any auras that your hero has will be carried through to your primary Split Brewling. The primary Split Brewling order goes from Earth -> Storm -> Fire. The primary Split Brewling's location is also where your hero will return to when the ult ends.
  • The Earth Brewling deals extra damage to structures and can receive buffs like Invoker's Alacrity or Ogre Magi's Bloodlust.
  • I don't care what the situation is, the Cyclone on the Storm Brewling will always be useful. Cyclone a support so that your teammates won't waste time killing that squishy Crystal Maiden when they should be focusing the carry. Cyclone that Omniknight so his whole team cries at him for not ulting. Cyclone that Phantom Assassin who is slicing everyone to shreds because the rest of her team is locking your allies down. Cyclone the roided up Sven and waste a whopping 6 seconds of God's Strength. Then, when he comes back down, wait two seconds for it to come off cooldown and Cyclone his punk ass again.
  • The Storm Brewling's Cyclone lasts the full duration even if Primal Split ends.
  • Be aware that unlike Eul's, the Storm Brewling's Cyclone does not deal damage on landing. So heroes can potentially Blink Dagger out immediately after landing. Not even the Fire Brewling's Immolation aura will damage enemies quickly enough to cancel a Blink Dagger.
  • Casting the Storm Brewling's Dispel Magic on the Cyclone will immediately cancel it. Don't stand around watching the enemy spin for the full Cyclone duration if your team is ready to kill them, just end it early.
    • Dispel Magic can also end an enemy self-Eul's.
  • Dispel Magic deals a ton of damage to all summoned units in the AoE excluding Lone Druid's Spirit Bear, Undying's Tombstone zombies, clones (ex: Meepos, Arc Warden's Tempest Double), and ward-type units (ex: Pugna's Nether Ward, Shadow Shaman's Serpent Wards).
    • Keep this mechanic in mind if you're against heroes that use illusions, like Naga Siren, Phantom Lancer, or Terrorblade. It even deals full damage to 'strong' illusions, like from Chaos Knight's Phantasm.
    • Heroes with a lot of summons like Broodmother, Enigma, Nature's Prophet, or Wraith King can also have their armies instagibbed by Dispel Magic.
  • Dispel Magic is also, obviously, a weak/basic dispel. Here's a long list of things you can basic dispel.
    • The dispel works on all enemies and allies in the AoE. So in addition to removing buffs off enemies, you can also remove things like silences, roots, or slows off your allies.
  • The Storm Brewling's Cyclone lasts extra long against lesser creeps/summons. This isn't really useful for anything other than maybe annoying a Chen.
  • The Storm Brewling's Wind Walk gives free bonus damage on its next attack. You should easily be able to use it multiple times during your ult if you pay attention to the cooldown. It's a lot of physical damage that you may have been missing out on.
    • Attacks during the fade time of the Wind Walk apply the bonus damage without losing the buff. So technically, you could get 4 Wind Walk damage hits in 2 Wind Walk casts. It's fairly easy to do, just make sure you're in range of the enemy you're attacking, cast the Wind Walk, and then immediately right click the enemy. The first attack will land with bonus damage without removing your invisibility.
  • The Wind Walk also gives a massive move speed increase on the Storm Brewling. Use it if you're trying to catch an escaping enemy. Once you catch up, cast the Cyclone.
  • Late game, Wind Walk will be your main escape mechanism because your Fire Brewling will go down as soon as someone so much as farts on it, and the Earth Brewling goes down surprisingly quickly once carries start doling out big single target damage.
  • Often times you'll be finishing your Primal Split only to have the entire enemy team staring hungrily at your Earth Brewling, waiting for your return. If you have a Blink Dagger, position your Earth Brewling where you can get an effective Blink, and start mashing the item hotkey. Just be sure to turn the Spirit to face the direction you want to Blink in before Primal Split ends. This will avoid any turn animation delay since Brewmaster faces the same direction that the Spirit was last facing.
  • In high ground siege situations, you can consider sacrificing your Earth Brewling into the enemy fountain after your Storm Brewling has already made a break for it to guarantee that you'll respawn in your Storm Brewling.
  • Brewling attacks count the same as illusions when calculating damage against ward-type units that require a fixed number of attacks to kill (ex: Pugna's Nether Ward, Shadow Shaman's Serpent Wards), which is usually half damage.

Counters

  • Kill the Fire Brewling ASAP in the early game. It's very squishy, but deals the majority of damage in Primal Split.
  • Unlike Eul's, the Storm Brewling's Cyclone does not apply any damage when it ends, so you can immediately Blink out once you land.
  • You can left click the Spirits and check their buffs to see how much time is remaining on the Split. This is relevant if you're trying to drop some damage/disables on the Earth Brewling to disable Brewmaster's Blink as he returns. You can alt+click the buff to announce the time left in chat for your team.
  • SILENSILSILSILESILENSILENCESILENCE. Dude hates silences and hexes. Primal Split has a cast time and hexes and some silences are instant. If you have these on your team he'll likely have to cut his Blink → Cinder Brew → Thunder Clap → Primal Split combo to just Blink → Primal Split.
  • It's worth taking the time to kill his Agh's Scepter Primal Companions if it's convenient. He can't resummon one for a long time if you successfully kill it.
