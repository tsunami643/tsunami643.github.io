Luna

Tips

  • The Lunar Blessing innate damage aura works on creep-hero units like Brewmaster's Primal Split Brewlings, Visage's Familiars, or Warlock's Golem.
  • The Lunar Blessing innate gives you bonus night vision. If you see an enemy at night just at the edge of your vision, there's a good chance you can see them but they can't see you.
  • Lunar Orbit deals damage in the form of multiple instant attacks, but behaves a little differently than other instant attacks. Here are a few rules on how it works:
    • Unlike most instant attacks, on-hit effects (like lifesteal, Basher, Maelstrom, or Desolator) will NOT apply during Lunar Orbit.
    • Unlike most instant attacks, you will NOT lose invisibility from Shadow Blade or Silver Edge or even Smoke if you damage a unit.
    • Lunar Orbit instant attacks will ignore disarms and have true strike.
    • Lunar Orbit instant attacks can damage invisible enemies that are in the AoE.
    • Lunar Orbit instant attacks can damage couriers.
  • Bounces from Moon Glaives don't actually behave as right clicks or instant attacks, but rather as a passive spell. Some rules on how Glaives operate:
    • Glaive bounces have true strike.
    • Glaive bounces won't proc on-hit effects like Centaur Warrunner's Retaliate or break enemy Tranquil Boots.
    • Lifesteal sources don't work with Glaives bounces.
    • Glaives are reduced by damage block.
  • Lunar Orbit and Moon Glaive bounces can kill Undying Tombstone zombies.
  • Eclipse is married to Lucent Beam. If for some reason you're keeping Lucent Beam at low levels, Eclipse is going to do less damage.
  • Eclipse doesn't continue after you die, so don't use it if you're getting ganked just because you're angry that you got caught.
    • Agh's Scepter Eclipse (ground or self targeted) ends prematurely if Luna dies. When cast on an ally, it ends prematurely if that ally dies.
  • Agh's Scepter Eclipse can be targeted on any allied unit except ward-type units. You can even target it on couriers.
  • A ground targeted Agh's Scepter Eclipse only provides a small radius of ground vision, not flying vision, around the center of the AoE. If enemies are hiding in fog behind trees, but inside the AoE, they won't get hit by the Beams.
  • Eclipse has a fairly long cast time. It's okay to cast it earlier than you need it just so you can get it off before getting perma-stunned.
  • Eclipse turns the map to night for 10 seconds. If you see a bunch of enemies moments away from ganking you and you really don't want to die, you can use Eclipse's night to potentially hide yourself from enemy vision.

Counters

  • Luna has one of the highest base move speeds in the game, so as tempting as it can be to dive her in the early game, it can be risky without disables.
  • Luna has bonus night vision thanks to her Lunar Blessing innate, so you need to be sneaky if you're trying to gank her at night.
  • Once your tier 3 tower goes down, get ready to enter Glaive City. Not only will the Glaives bounce through all your base's structures and damage everything in sight, but there is a high probability that your Blink Dagger is going to get disabled from an errant Glaive. Stand as far back as possible from your structures if you're planning on Blink initiating.
  • Luna's Moon Glaives passive and Lunar Blessing innate can both be disabled by breaks.
    • Breaks can be provided with items like Silver Edge or Khanda. Spells that Break include Doom's Agh's Scepter Doom, Hoodwink's Sharpshooter, Naga Siren's Agh's Scepter Ensnare, Nyx Assassin's Vendetta, Phantom Assassin's Agh's Scepter Fan of Knives, Shadow Demon's Agh's Scepter Demonic Purge, Viper's Viper Strike, and a few others.
  • Eclipse can be a terrifying spell, but there are ways to deal with it by having more units in the AoE to tank Beams. Summons and illusions good ways to mitigate the risk of eating Beams. Blade Mail could potentially kill her if you're tanky.
