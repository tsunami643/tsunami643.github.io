Venomancer

Tips

  • Snakebite deals burst damage on the enemy every time they attack. Here are a few enemies that it really annoys:
    • Heroes with spells that deal multiple "instant attacks" like Dawnbreaker's Starbreaker, Hoodwink's Acorn Shot, Kez's Echo Slash, or Pangolier's Swashbuckle.
    • Heroes that can't easily stop themselves from attacking like Legion Commander's Duel, Razor's Static Link, Windranger's Focus Fire, Weaver's Geminate Attack, or even Jakiro with his Double Trouble two attacks innate.
    • Heroes that have short bursts of high attack speed like Phantom Assassin's Phantom Strike or Ursa's Overpower.
  • You can cast Snakebite on Rosh.
  • Are you struggling at maintaining creep equilibrium in the laning phase because of your Plague Wards? Watch this instructional video to help solve that problem.
  • tl;dw - Use the 'Cancel Current Action'/stop key and use unified unit orders. No one is forcing Plague Wards to constantly attack. Stop them if there are no heroes around, sic 'em all on a creep at the same time to last hit or deny, put them up on cliffs and tell them to shut up so they don't alert an enemy who didn't know you could see them.
    • The "Unified Orders with CTRL" setting is also useful when chasing enemies down with Plague Wards. When you throw one up ahead, don't waste time box-selecting, just issue a unified ctrl+right click on the enemy hero.
  • Plague Ward attacks apply a half-damage Poison Sting innate debuff, but the slow is the same value as your hero's Poison Sting innate.
  • Plague Wards have collision and can be used to block narrow paths.
  • Try to deny your Plague Wards in lane. Use your other Plague Wards to help deny dying Plague Wards.
  • Spam Plague Wards for vision. Seriously. Just slap them everywhere. They're super annoying for enemies to deal with and have 800 day/night vision.
  • When jungling, try to place your Plague Wards outside of the spawn box so you don't block new camps from spawning. Plague Wards last an eternity and will probably not time out before a camp respawns.
    • Alternatively, Plague Wards are great for blocking enemy camps.
  • The secondary Noxious Plague projectiles can spread to invisible and fogged enemies.
  • When spread Noxious Plague projectiles are launched, they will carry all debuffs that Veno has currently applied on the target and duplicate them on the secondary target(s).
    • For example, if you have your Poison Sting innate and Venomous Gale currently ticking on the primary Noxious Plague target, the spread projectiles will copy the duration left on those debuffs and apply them to all targets hit by the secondary Noxious Plague.
    • This even applies to item debuffs. If you have Urn of Shadows or even a Scythe of Vyse with 1.25 seconds left applied to the Noxious Plague target, the spread projectiles will also apply an Urn of Shadows and 1.25 second Scythe of Vyse on all targets hit.
  • Refreshers are pretty useless on Veno since all his debuffs are only refreshed when reapplied, they don't stack.

Counters

  • Veno's Poison Sting innate damage over time is classed as "HP removal" so it does not cancel Blink Daggers or consumable healing. You can use a Salve/Clarity/Bottle without fear of it getting canceled.
  • You can deny allies debuffed by Venomous Gale.
  • All of Veno's spell debuffs can be removed with basic dispels (like from activating Manta Style or Lotus Orb). His Poison Sting innate, Venomous Gale, Snakebite, and Noxious Plague are all dispellable.
    • Noxious Plague will immediately spread when dispelled.
    • Veno has a "Snakebite Undispellable" talent.
  • You can't disjoint the initial or the secondary Noxious Plague projectiles.
  • If you're panicking in your fountain because you think you're about to die to Veno's damage, consider a few options:
    • Buy a Faerie Fire.
    • Buy Blood Grenades.
    • Buy a Shawl if you have the gold. You can sell it back a few seconds later (but it turns your gold unreliable).
    • Pray.
