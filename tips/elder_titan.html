Elder Titan

Tips

  • If you Echo Stomp while the Spirit isn't out, both the magical and physical damage instances will work off your hero.
  • Echo Stomped targets will wake up if they take a certain amount of total damage during the sleep or when the debuff times out.
    • Enemies have to take actual damage on their HP to count towards the wake threshold. So things like barriers or Medusa's Mana Shield will increase the amount of "damage" they can take before waking up.
    • Don't always start beating on an enemy as soon as you land an Echo Stomp sleep otherwise you may wake them up before your team has gotten into a better position.
  • You can cast Echo Stomp when the Spirit is flying back to you on its way home. Cast the Spirit, step on some fools, then have it walk further away so the enemy team will forget about it. Then when it's on the way back, cast Echo Stomp and remind the enemies that your Spirit's still around. Since the Spirit returns way faster than it walks normally, enemies won't always react quick enough to dodge it.
  • If you spam cancel Echo Stomp's cast animation from your hero with your 'Cancel Current Action'/stop hotkey while the Spirit is returning, you can slightly extend the time it takes for the Spirit to return to your hero.
  • With Agh's Shard Echo Stomp you could cast your Spirit, TP back home for regen/shopping, and then cast Echo Stomp on alt-cast to return back on the map.
  • The Spirit's move speed is based on ET's at the time of casting. This means that a Dark Seer Surge'd ET will have a Surge-speed Spirit.
    • This makes Phase Boots or Boots of Bearing quite effective on ET since you can activate the bonus move speed and then cast Spirit and have it roam around at higher speeds for its entire duration.
  • Natural Order armor reduction aura is centered around the hero, and the magical reduction aura is centered around the Spirit.
  • Natural Order reduces base armor and magic resistance, which comes from AGI and INT respectively. It will not reduce armor or magic resistance granted by items (unless the items are adding resistances in the form of +AGI or +INT).
  • You can select your Astral Spirit to control it as a separate unit.
    • You can right click an enemy when your Spirit is selected to have it follow the target for its duration.
    • There is no difference between casting Echo Stomp from your Spirit or from your hero. However, if your hero is taunted, you can cast Echo Stomp from your Spirit and then both your hero and the Spirit will start channeling the spell. This only works when taunted, not under stuns, silences, sleeps, or fears.
  • Having your Spirit sit around on enemies in lane just for Natural Order is useless if you're not planning on attacking them any time soon. Have your Spirit go suck up some more damage/farm by sending it into jungle camps rather than sitting around cuddling heroes.
    • The Spirit can take Twin Gates. It takes forever for it to come back from its semester abroad, but it may step on a lot of damage on the way back.
  • You can use a long-distance Spirit Return to your advantage to set up for kills. Spirit starts cooling down whenever it's cast, not whenever it's Returned. When the Spirit finally returns, start pounding on people with the extra bonus damage and you'll have another Spirit cooldown ready in the chamber to finish them off.
    • Whatever Spirit bonus buffs you have are immediately replaced by new values when returning a new Spirit.
  • If the Astral Spirit steps on multiple clone heroes, like Meepos or Arc Warden + Tempest Double, each individual clone adds bonus hero damage to the Spirit.
  • It can be in your benefit to stack neutral camps near your lane so you can gain a lot of damage by running your Spirit over more creeps.
  • If your hero is within ~1800 range of a neutral camp, you can draw aggro and stack it with Astral Spirit.
  • Use the Spirit to scout out fogged areas during pushes or defenses.
  • During chases, try to pincer your target by casting the Spirit on a choke point. Either they have to run through the whole thing and risk getting Stomped or they have to change directions.
  • Ideally, you want to cast Earth Splitter as far away from the enemy team as possible to give them the smallest amount of time to react to the crack approaching.

Counters

  • With shpeed. Elder Titan is a very unwieldy hero. His Stomp is slow, his Spirit is slow, Earth Splitter is slow. If you can move fast and avoid his spells, you can evade a lot of damage that ET should be dealing to you.
    • Along those lines, Force Staff gives you an easy eject button if you're getting comboed into some of his spells.
  • The Astral Spirit buff can be dispelled off of Elder Titan. This can make heroes like Enchantress with Enchant, Largo with Catchy Lick, or Oracle with Fortune's End handy in the laning phase.
    • Techies with Reactive Tazer also dispels enemies who attack a buffed target which can be useful to discourage Elder Titan from hitting your allies.
  • Oracle can wake allies up from Echo Stomp with Purifying Flames damage. Pudge can wake himself up with Rot.
  • Elder Titans will often stack camps in lane just so they can get more Spirit damage. Try to interfere with his stacks if he's doing this.
