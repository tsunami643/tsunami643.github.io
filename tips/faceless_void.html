Faceless Void

Tips

  • You're invulnerable when flying in Time Walk. You can use this to run head first into projectiles like Gyrocopter's Homing Missile to negate the stun/damage. If your timing is godlike, you can even use the travel invulnerability to dodge things like the AoE stun from Phoenix's Supernova.
  • Tread switching is especially important to utilize on Void. Time Walk restores flat HP, so you stand to benefit a lot by switching to INT Treads for the mana and going back to STR/AGI after rewinding damage.
  • Time Dilation makes Void a very annoying counter for heroes with low cooldown or combo spells like, Timbersaw, Bristleback, Invoker, Lina, Kunkka, Weaver, Queen of Pain, all the Spirit heroes, Terrorblade, Phantom Assassin, and Slark (as long as he doesn't Dark Pact the debuff off first).
  • Time Dilation fully stacks if cast again on an enemy who's already Dilated.
  • Time Lock attacks can proc anything that your normal right clicks do, including another Time Lock.
  • Basher/Abyssal passive bashes don't work with Faceless Void. The game won't allow you to have another bash besides Time Lock.
  • Chronosphere provides true sight over its entire AoE. If an enemy decided to stop fighting you and Shadow Bladed out, cast a Chrono along the path and laugh when you discover them through invis without any sort of detection.
    • Observer/Sentry Wards will also be revealed when caught inside Chrono.
  • Donkeys in the Dota universe have a stronger grip on space and time than most heroes. Chronosphere will not freeze enemy couriers.
  • Any units owned by you can move around in a Chronosphere, such as illusions or Dominated creeps.
  • Many ward units act differently when interacting with Chrono:
    • Ward units that attack, like Beastmaster's Raptor Hawk, Clinkz's Skeleton Archers, Shadow Shaman's Mass Serpent Wards, Witch Doctor's Death Ward, Undying's Tombstone zombies, and Venomancer's Plague Wards are frozen if placed inside Chrono.
    • Clockwerk's Power Cogs will freeze when hit into the edge of a Chrono. However, once a Cog is already affected by Chrono, Clock can hit it again and it'll fly through the Chrono.
    • Gyrocopter's Homing Missile will die if it touches a Chrono.
    • Techies' Sticky Bomb will just straight up vanish if you cast it into a Chrono, however, it can drive into a Chrono just fine if you catch a hero on the edge.
    • Weaver Swarm bugs will instantly fall off on any targets affected by Chrono.
  • You don't have to be super worried about letting Rubick steal Chronosphere since Faceless Void has a unique mechanic that allows him to move in any Chronosphere regardless of who cast it.

Counters

  • The HP Faceless Void heals with Time Walk can be reduced by debuffs that affect health restoration:
    • Items that can reduce health restoration: Spirit Vessel, Orb of Frost/Corrosion, Eye of Skadi, Crella's Crozier, as well as the "Crippling Crossbow" and "Jidi Pollen Bag" neutral item artifacts.
    • Spells that can reduce health restoration: Abaddon's Withering Mist innate, Ancient Apparition's Ice Blast, Doom's Doom, Drow Ranger's Agh's Scepter Hypothermia, Pudge's Agh's Scepter Rot, Slark's Saltwater Shiv, and a few others.
  • Faceless Void cannot Time Walk when rooted.
  • The Time Dilation debuff can be dispelled.
  • Eul's is one of the cheapest counters to Chronosphere. You should be able to waste a good 2.5s of Chrono time by Cycloning him if you're outside the Chrono or you can use it on yourself if you're about to be Chrono'd.
    • Wind Waker can also be used to protect an ally in Chrono. However, allies can't move themselves while cycloned. Only a self-cast Wind Waker allows that.
  • Ghost Scepter can minimize the amount of physical damage you tank in a Chronosphere and later turning it into an Ethereal Blade can help save allies from dying to right clicks in a Chrono.
  • Void can freely walk through any Chronosphere. Keep this in mind if you're playing Rubick or trying to be tricky in Ability Draft.
    • This means that enemy Void illusions created by Dark Seer's Wall of Replica or Shadow Demon's Disruption can also move around in Chronosphere.
  • Many ward units act differently when interacting with Chrono:
    • Ward units that attack, like Beastmaster's Raptor Hawk, Clinkz's Skeleton Archers, Shadow Shaman's Mass Serpent Wards, Witch Doctor's Death Ward, Undying's Tombstone zombies, and Venomancer's Plague Wards are frozen if placed inside Chrono.
    • Clockwerk's Power Cogs will freeze when hit into the edge of a Chrono. However, once a Cog is already affected by Chrono, Clock can hit it again and it'll fly through the Chrono.
    • Gyrocopter's Homing Missile will die if it touches a Chrono.
    • Techies' Sticky Bomb will just straight up vanish if you cast it into a Chrono, however, it can drive into a Chrono just fine if you catch a hero on the edge.
    • Weaver Swarm bugs will instantly fall off on any targets affected by Chrono.
  • Monkey King's Wukong's Command soldiers work in any Chronosphere, allied or enemy.
  • Despite its appearance, Chronosphere is actually a giant cylinder, not a sphere. You can't pass over it with spells that use the z-axis, like Tiny's Toss. Units will just get stuck on the edge, but in the sky.
  • Buffs that provide invulnerability allow you to pass through a Chronosphere unaffected. This includes Centaur Warrunner's Agh's Scepter Hitch A Ride's passenger, Dazzle's Nothl Projection, Earth Spirit's Rolling Boulder, Morphling's Waveform, or Storm Spirit's Ball Lightning.
  • Faceless Void's "Backtrack" talent can be disabled by breaks. It cannot be bypassed by accuracy, like from MKB, because it's not evasion. It's just a chance to dodge any type of damage.
