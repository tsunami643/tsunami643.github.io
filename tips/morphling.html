Morphling

Tips

  • Use Waveform to travel between and damage two jungle camps at the same time.
  • Waveform can be used to absorb certain projectiles headfirst since you are invincible during the spell.
  • When using Waveform to push the lane, move near the ranged creep in the enemy wave and Waveform in towards your creeps or into trees. You make yourself vulnerable to ganks if you Waveform deeper into enemy territory.
  • You can toggle Morph STR/AGI while stunned/disabled and while channeling a TP.
  • Replicate has a surprisingly high cast range. Use this not only to make Replicas of enemies from far away during team fights, but as a gap closer during ganks (create a Replica, immediately take its position, you are now in your target) or as an escape when getting ganked.
  • When using a Replica to escape, treat it like an Illusory Orb. Let your hero walk in one direction and let the Replica go the opposite direction. Doesn't matter which one the enemies commit to, you're gold ponyboy.
  • If you're farming the jungle and you've got nothing better to do, micro your Replicate. Besides letting it get last hits for you, you can try sending it into enemy territory to bait out some ultimates.
  • Replicas deal 80% damage. An 80% damage Agh Tiny still hurts like crazy, whether he's the enemy or your ally. Find the highest damage dealer in the map and Replicate them for a team fight. Replicas also take 100% damage. A 100% HP Centaur takes an eternity to kill. If the highest damage dealer in the map is also someone who gets iced out in moments (i.e. Clinkz), then try to go for the tankiest.
  • Other good targets for Replication are heroes who have good passives or auras which carry over to illusions: Axe for Counter Helix, Phantom Lancer for your own personal PL army, Spectre for Desolate, Riki for Permanent Invisibility, and heroes who have a Radiance.
  • Mechanics on Hybrids:
    • Hybrids look distinct so the enemy can distinguish it from the original hero.
    • Hybrids take Morphling's STR, AGI, and INT stats on cast, not the hero's. These stats determine the Hybrid's base HP/Armor/Mana. However, movespeed/BAT/cast animation and other stats are from the hero itself.
    • This means that if you have a ton of STR morphed and you Hybridize a STR hero, your Hybrid will benefit from Morph's high STR stats (HP and damage will come from STR). Other way for an AGI hero Hybrid (attack speed, armor, and damage will come from AGI).
    • Hybrids copy the current percentage of HP/Mana remaining on the base hero when cast.
    • Hybrids follow all illusion rules regarding attack modifiers, auras, and passives. They can also be instakilled like any other illusion by Hex.
    • Hybrids can't use item actives.
    • This includes Aghanim's Scepter. If you make a Hybrid of an ally that has an Agh upgraded basic spell (Sand King's Burrowstrike, for example) then the Hybrid will have the Agh upgraded version as well.
    • However, spells granted by an Agh will not be usable. No Treant Eyes or Nimbus casting Hybrids allowed.
    • Hybrids spawn with a full set of ES Remnant and Shrapnel charges, but only one Spirit Siphon and Spin Web charge.
    • A Hybrid timing out behaves the same way as Rubick's Spell Steal timing out in terms of spells/summons lingering.
    • If you Hybridize an ally that's in a Transformation, your Hybrid will spawn Transformed. The bonuses are massively inconsistent, though, and not worth listing.
    • Hybrid can be Refreshed and you can have two active Hybrids out at the same time.

Counters

  • Morphling cannot Waveform when rooted.
  • You need to have nukes if you want to kill Morph. With STR Morph, he can usually gain HP before you can burn him down with auto attacks.
  • You can see how much time Morphling has left on a Replicate illusion by checking his buff timer. Time your ganks when it's about to end.
  • Be wary of committing fully to out of position heroes when there's a Morphling on the enemy team. It's highly likely that it's a Replicate.
  • If you built a Radiance, try to keep it toggled off if you see Morph hanging around trying to Replicate you.
  • If Morph went E-Blade, he's probably roaming around with low strength, making him a prime candidate for a good old fashioned Nyxing. Good Nyxers include: Nyx, Nyx, Nyx, Io+Anyone, Nyx, and Dagon 5 + Sheepstick Tinker or Furion.
