Mars

Tips

  • Spear of Mars provides 300 flying vision as it travels and the vision lingers around an impaled enemy.
  • The Spear does not ignore illusions, it'll skewer them just like a normal hero.
  • Forced movement can shift enemies around after they get skewered, but as long as the Spear still connects to a wall/tree, the enemy will still get stunned wherever they are. God's Rebuke counts as forced movement, so if you're quick, you can Rebuke a target while they're flying back from your Spear to cancel the Spear movement.
  • However, keep in mind that applying any forced movement during the Spear will also briefly remove the stun debuff from an enemy until the Spear hits a wall. So if you're against enemies with instant cast spells or items like BKB/Eul's, they will be able to activate the spell/item during the Rebuke bump.
  • Tusk's Ice Shards, Clockwerk's Power Cogs, Nature's Prophet's Sprout, and obviously your own Arena of Blood's walls count as objects that you can pin an enemy to with your Spear to get the stun. Earthshaker's Fissure does not count as of this patch.
  • Iron Branch trees also count, but the collision with the Spear isn't very consistent.
  • The sides of Rosh's pit do not count as walls, only the back of his pit counts. However, there are a few trees surrounding his pit, so aim accordingly if you want to go for the skewering stun, or if you just want to send an enemy flying out of the pit.
  • Only the primary target of the Spear that is skewered gets stunned. The knockback on other enemies in the path of the Spear does not count as a disable.
  • If you're extremely quick fingered, you can cast Arena while an enemy is flying from your Spear and pin them to the Arena wall. Works most consistently if you're maxing out the cast range of the Arena.
  • Invisible units can get Speared, but you'll only get true sight on the target if they get impaled on something.
  • The impact particle effect from God's Rebuke can indirectly show where an invisible unit is.
  • God's Rebuke's bump can't push enemies out of Disruptor's Kinetic Field, but Spear of Mars will yeet enemies out of the Field.
  • The knockback on God's Rebuke does not count as a disable and will not cancel enemy channels.
  • Damage from God's Rebuke can go through spell immunity, but the knockback won't.
  • God's Rebuke performs a true strike instant attack on all targets in the AoE. This means each target has a chance to proc/apply attack modifiers, excluding cleave. Just keep in mind that most chance based procs from items have an internal cooldown and can't proc multiple targets simultaneously.
  • God's Rebuke can kill enemy couriers.
  • Since God's Rebuke includes a 'right click,' it'll break your Smoke if it damages a unit.
  • Bulwark only reduces damage from attacks and instant attacks, but not physical damage spells.
  • When Bulwark is toggled on, any ranged attack projectile launched to a target in a cone behind Mars has a percentage chance to travel straight to Mars instead of their original intended targets. This works against projectiles from Medusa's Split Shot or Gyrocopter's Flak Cannon.
    • You can also redirect attacks with active Bulwark by standing within 100 units of the ally being targeted. The angle of the source attack does not matter in this case.
  • Toggling Bulwark on will also redirect attacks from towers, including the multishots from Glyphed towers. Active Bulwark also redirects attacks from ward-type units, like Shadow Shaman's Serpent Wards or Witch Doctor's Death Ward.
  • Toggling Bulwark can prevent Rubick from stealing your better spells.
  • You can toggle Bulwark while channeling a TP.
  • When Bulwark is toggled on, you don't have to turn to cast items. This includes items targetable on enemies, like Eul's, Force Staff, Medallion, Urn of Shadows, etc.
  • Normally, casting a TP scroll will turn your hero to the direction of the structure you're traveling to. But since you don't have to turn to cast items when Bulwark is on, you can also avoid turning to your TP target. This can be useful if you're escaping and TP'ing to fountain. Face your attackers, toggle Bulwark on, then channel the TP to benefit from Bulwark's damage reduction while TP'ing in the opposite direction.
  • Not having to turn for items can be especially useful if you have a Blink and are getting Eul's combo'd and you have a way to mitigate the landing damage of the Cyclone (like Hood, Pipe, Eternal Shroud). While you're cycloned, activate the Hood/Pipe, shift+queue Bulwark's active, then shift+queue your Blink Dagger and you should be able to instantly Blink out without having to turn.
  • Agh's Scepter Bulwark soldiers attack once per second. The one second interval is calculated only while Bulwark is toggled on. You can somewhat abuse this by toggling Bulwark on during the 'backswing' interval, then toggling off before the soldiers are about to attack. Then the next time you toggle them on, they'll attack immediately, and then reset again for another full second.
  • Agh's Scepter Bulwark soldiers can proc any attack modifiers your hero has. They can also attack buildings.
  • Agh's Scepter Bulwark soldiers will not attack if you are stunned or disarmed.
  • Agh's Scepter Bulwark soldiers are actual units with actual vision. This means that if you can maneuver one of them into the Rosh pit or on a high ground, they can provide roughly 800 vision that your hero couldn't naturally see.
  • Arena of Blood blocks enemy attack projectiles from entering the ring. This includes attacks from towers or the fountain.
  • If a spell immune ranged enemy is about to kill you during the Arena, weave in and out of the Arena walls. The enemy hero may be able to pass through the walls, but their attack projectiles still get blocked.
  • Here are the rules of engagement when dealing with the Arena of Blood:
    • Enemies can freely blink in or out of the Arena. You should initiate against Blink Dagger carriers by hitting them with God's Rebuke first to break their Dagger so they can't just Blink out of your Arena.
    • Enemies with movement spells that provide invulnerability during travel (like Faceless Void's Time Walk, Morphling's Waveform, or Storm Spirit's Ball Lightning, Wind Waker's active Cyclone) can freely pass through the Arena walls.
    • Spell immunity allows enemies to pass freely through the Arena walls.
    • Spells that provide flying movement, unobstructed movement, or otherwise modify a hero's z-axis do not allow passage over the Arena walls.
    • Heroes can utilize forced movement spells to shove targets into your Arena, like with Earth Spirit's Boulder Smash or Keeper of the Light's Blinding Light, but they can't use those spells to take people out of the Arena.
    • Force Staff cannot push enemies in or out of the Arena.
    • Pudge can Meat Hook enemy heroes in or out of the Arena. When Hooking them out, they'll still bump into the Arena wall, but Meat Hook always brings targets to the Hook location once it ends.
    • Enemy Clockwerks can Hookshot into the Arena, but cannot Hookshot themselves out of the Arena. Either direction, he'll still get canceled by the wall soldier (if it's not on cooldown) before he reaches his target.
    • The Arena wall soldiers will attack invisible units regardless of detection.
    • The wall soldier knockback is non-disabling, so Force Staffing a TP'ing enemy into the wall won't cancel their channel.

Counters

  • I advise scrolling up and reading through the Arena rules to figure out what does and doesn't work to get out of it. Quick reference is blinks (Phantom Assassin's Phantom Strike, Riki's Blink Strike, actual Blinks), invulnerable movement spells (Faceless Void's Time Walk, Morphling's Waveform, Storm Spirit's Ball Lightning), and spell immunity (Juggernaut's Blade Fury, Lifestealer's Rage, building BKB) allows you to pass through the Arena.
  • Bulwark can be disabled with Breaks.
  • Heroes dealing high magic damage are invaluable since Bulwark only reduces right clicks.
  • Magic damage can also penetrate the Arena of Blood, whereas right click projectiles will be blocked by the wall.
  • If you have a Quelling Blade/Battle Fury or Tango, you can try to go for big plays by lining yourself up with a tree for him to Spear you against and then destroying the tree before the Spear connects.
